Coca-Cola

Chuck Wagon occupies a tiny space in downtown Wilmette. However, it turns out a high volume of burgers, gyros, dogs and fries, especially at lunchtime. Chuck Wagon redesigns American classics with one simple ingredient: slices of succulent gyro, straight from the spit. With only about 12 seats, Chuck Wagon has a quite unique and refreshing atmosphere. They sure do love their customers as nearly every inch of wall space is filled with their photographs that the owner takes himself.

SpongeBob SquarePants

Depot Nuevo is a Mexican restaurant and tequila bar with a bright and lively atmosphere. The menu at Depot Nuevo is distinctly modern Mexican. There are several varieties of tacos, enchiladas, burrito and fajitas along with the traditional appetizers and dinner salads. The specialty of the house, though, is the paella, with saffron rice and shrimp, bay scallops, mussels, chicken and chorizo. All the margaritas are created from scratch with a house made sour mix and fresh-squeezed fruit for the traditional lime, strawberry, and raspberry flavors.

Marvel

Since 1975, Irving's for Red Hot Lovers has established itself as a local legend in the Chicagoland area. Here you can find the classic Chicago-styled hot dog, also known as a red hot. An authentic Chicago red hot is topped with yellow mustard, chopped white onions, bright green sweet pickle relish, a dill pickle spear, tomato slices or wedges, pickled sport peppers, and a dash of celery salt. If you're up for the challenge, head over to Irving's for Red Hot Lovers.
